JiKi: The AI Kimono Sensei Instructor

Preserving Traditional Kimono
Craftsmanship through AI

Purpose

This project introduces JiKi, an AI-based system designed to train and evaluate learners worldwide in the art of kimono making, preserving traditional craftsmanship.

Overview

JiKi is an innovative AI platform that trains students from beginner to expert level. It teaches kimono-making techniques while reinforcing Japanese culture and language.

Background

The kimono evolved from the kosode during the Edo period. While fashion remained stable, modernization reduced its use. Today, few masters remain, and JiKi aims to preserve their knowledge.

How JiKi Works

  • AI learns from a Kimono Sensei
  • Student wears JiBand arm devices
  • Student practices drawing kimono flowers
  • AI evaluates and corrects performance
  • Progress is stored and shared

Technology & Training

Students collaborate internationally using skills such as Python, Java, machine learning, and data science. Teams also develop wearable JiBand devices.

Next Steps

  • Recruit student engineers
  • Design and test JiBands
  • Train AI with expert data
  • Distribute kits to students
  • Launch global AI training
